ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY (AUP)
TelecomCo – Internal Policy Document
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Version: 3.2
Classification: Internal

1. PURPOSE
This Acceptable Use Policy defines the standards for responsible use of TelecomCo's
network infrastructure, corporate devices, and digital services. All employees,
contractors, and third-party vendors with access to company resources must comply.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Contractors and consultants with network access
- Third-party vendors using TelecomCo systems
- All company-owned or managed devices and networks

3. PERMITTED USE
3.1 Employees may use company devices and network access for:
    - Performing job-related tasks and communications
    - Accessing approved cloud services (Microsoft 365, Salesforce, ServiceNow)
    - Limited personal use during non-work hours, provided it does not violate this policy

3.2 Approved communication channels include:
    - Corporate email (Outlook)
    - Microsoft Teams for internal collaboration
    - Approved CRM systems for customer interactions

4. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES
The following activities are strictly prohibited on TelecomCo networks and devices:
4.1 Unauthorized access to customer data or billing systems
4.2 Installing unapproved software or browser extensions
4.3 Sharing login credentials with any person, including colleagues
4.4 Using company resources for personal commercial ventures
4.5 Downloading or distributing copyrighted material without authorization
4.6 Circumventing network security controls (VPN bypasses, proxy tools)
4.7 Connecting unauthorized personal devices to the corporate network
4.8 Sending bulk unsolicited communications (spam) via company systems

5. MONITORING AND ENFORCEMENT
5.1 TelecomCo reserves the right to monitor all network traffic, email
    communications, and device activity on company-owned resources.
5.2 Monitoring is conducted in compliance with applicable privacy laws.
5.3 Violations will be investigated by the Information Security team.

6. CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION
6.1 First Offense: Written warning and mandatory policy training
6.2 Second Offense: Suspension of network access (24-72 hours) and manager notification
6.3 Third Offense: Termination review by HR and potential legal action
6.4 Severe Violations: Immediate suspension and investigation; may result in
    termination and referral to law enforcement.

7. BYOD (BRING YOUR OWN DEVICE) GUIDELINES
7.1 Personal devices may only connect to the "TelecomCo-Guest" Wi-Fi network.
7.2 To access corporate email on a personal device, employees must:
    - Enroll in Mobile Device Management (MDM)
    - Enable device encryption
    - Set a minimum 6-digit PIN or biometric lock
7.3 IT reserves the right to remotely wipe corporate data from personal devices
    if the device is lost, stolen, or the employee is terminated.

8. REPORTING
Employees who suspect a policy violation must report it to:
- Information Security: infosec@telecomco.com
- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-ETHIC (anonymous)
- Direct supervisor (for non-urgent matters)

Reports must be filed within 24 hours of discovering the violation.
