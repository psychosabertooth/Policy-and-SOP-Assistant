CUSTOMER DATA HANDLING & PRIVACY POLICY
TelecomCo – Internal Policy Document
Effective Date: March 1, 2025
Version: 2.1
Classification: Confidential

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes mandatory requirements for the collection, storage,
processing, and disposal of customer data across all TelecomCo operations.
It ensures compliance with federal regulations (CCPA, state privacy laws)
and industry standards (PCI-DSS for payment data).

2. DATA CLASSIFICATION
TelecomCo classifies customer data into the following categories:

2.1 Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
    - Full name, date of birth, Social Security Number
    - Home address, email address, phone number
    - Government-issued ID numbers

2.2 Customer Proprietary Network Information (CPNI)
    - Call detail records (numbers called, duration, time)
    - Service usage data and patterns
    - Billing amounts and payment history

2.3 Payment Card Information (PCI)
    - Credit/debit card numbers
    - Expiration dates, CVV codes
    - Cardholder name and billing address

2.4 Account Information
    - Account numbers and PINs
    - Service plans and features
    - Device information (IMEI, serial numbers)

3. ACCESS CONTROLS
3.1 Access to customer data is granted on a need-to-know basis only.
3.2 All access requests must be approved by the employee's manager AND
    the Data Privacy Officer (DPO).
3.3 Access is provisioned through role-based access control (RBAC) in
    the Identity Management System (IMS).
3.4 Customer-facing agents may access only the data required for the
    specific interaction (e.g., billing inquiry, service change).
3.5 Access to CPNI requires additional CPNI training certification,
    renewed annually.

4. DATA HANDLING PROCEDURES
4.1 Collection:
    - Collect only the minimum data necessary for the business purpose.
    - Inform customers about data collection at the point of collection.
    - Obtain explicit consent for marketing use of customer data.

4.2 Storage:
    - All customer data must be stored in approved systems only
      (Oracle CRM, Salesforce, approved databases).
    - Data must NOT be stored in personal drives, email attachments,
      spreadsheets, or unauthorized cloud services.
    - Encryption at rest is mandatory (AES-256 minimum).

4.3 Transmission:
    - Customer data must be transmitted over encrypted channels only (TLS 1.2+).
    - Email containing PII must be sent via the secure email gateway.
    - Never include full SSN, card numbers, or account PINs in email or chat.

4.4 Retention:
    - Active customer data: Retained for duration of service + 3 years.
    - Billing records: 7 years (per FCC requirements).
    - Call detail records: 18 months (per legal retention schedule).
    - Marketing consent records: Duration of consent + 2 years.

4.5 Disposal:
    - Electronic data: Cryptographic erasure or DOD 5220.22-M wipe.
    - Physical documents: Cross-cut shredding (DIN Level P-4 minimum).
    - Disposal must be logged in the Data Disposal Register.

5. CUSTOMER RIGHTS
5.1 Customers have the right to:
    - Request access to their personal data (response within 30 days).
    - Request deletion of their data ("Right to Delete").
    - Opt out of data sales (TelecomCo does NOT sell customer data).
    - Receive a copy of their data in portable format.

5.2 Customer requests must be processed through the Privacy Request Portal.
5.3 Identity verification is required before fulfilling any data request.

6. BREACH NOTIFICATION
6.1 Any suspected data breach must be reported to the Security Operations
    Center (SOC) within 1 hour of discovery.
6.2 SOC contact: soc@telecomco.com | 1-800-555-SECU (24/7)
6.3 The Incident Response Team will assess the breach within 4 hours.
6.4 If customer data is confirmed compromised:
    - Affected customers notified within 72 hours
    - State attorneys general notified as required by state law
    - FCC notified within 30 days for breaches affecting 5,000+ customers

7. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
7.1 All employees: Annual Data Privacy Awareness training.
7.2 Customer-facing staff: Quarterly CPNI handling refresher.
7.3 IT and Data teams: Annual PCI-DSS compliance training.
7.4 Training completion is tracked in the Learning Management System (LMS).

8. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE
8.1 Unintentional violation: Retraining + documented warning.
8.2 Negligent handling: Suspension of data access + performance review.
8.3 Intentional misuse: Termination + potential criminal referral.
8.4 Regulatory fines resulting from employee negligence may be reflected
    in performance evaluations.
