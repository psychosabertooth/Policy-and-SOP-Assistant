NETWORK OUTAGE RESPONSE – STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
TelecomCo – SOP Document
Document ID: SOP-NOC-2025-001
Effective Date: January 15, 2025
Version: 4.0
Classification: Internal

1. PURPOSE
This SOP provides step-by-step procedures for Network Operations Center (NOC)
engineers and field technicians to follow when a network outage is detected or
reported. It ensures consistent, rapid response to minimize service disruption.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all outages affecting:
- Wireless network (4G LTE / 5G)
- Fiber and DSL broadband services
- Enterprise and wholesale circuits
- Core network infrastructure (routers, switches, aggregation points)

3. SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION

Severity 1 (Critical):
- Complete service loss affecting >10,000 customers
- Core network element failure
- Multiple cell sites down in a metro area
- Response time: Immediate (within 15 minutes)
- Resolution target: 4 hours

Severity 2 (Major):
- Partial service degradation affecting 1,000-10,000 customers
- Single aggregation node failure
- Significant packet loss or latency (>200ms)
- Response time: Within 30 minutes
- Resolution target: 8 hours

Severity 3 (Minor):
- Localized issue affecting <1,000 customers
- Single cell site outage
- Non-critical system alert
- Response time: Within 2 hours
- Resolution target: 24 hours

Severity 4 (Informational):
- Planned maintenance window
- Minor performance anomaly
- Proactive alert with no customer impact
- Response time: Next business day
- Resolution target: As scheduled

4. DETECTION AND INITIAL RESPONSE

Step 1: Alert Triage
- NOC monitoring systems (SolarWinds, Netcool) generate alerts automatically.
- All alerts appear on the NOC dashboard and are logged in ServiceNow.
- On-call engineer reviews alert within the response time for its severity.

Step 2: Initial Assessment
- Verify the alert is genuine (not a false positive from monitoring flap).
- Check for related alerts that might indicate a larger issue.
- Confirm customer impact using the Customer Impact Dashboard.
- Document initial findings in the ServiceNow incident ticket.

Step 3: Classify and Escalate
- Assign severity level based on criteria in Section 3.
- For Severity 1: Immediately notify the NOC Manager and initiate a bridge call.
- For Severity 2: Notify the NOC Manager within 15 minutes.
- For Severity 3-4: Follow standard ticket workflow.

5. TROUBLESHOOTING PROCEDURE

Step 4: Isolate the Problem
- Determine if the issue is in the access, aggregation, or core layer.
- Check for recent changes in the Change Management system (was a change
  deployed in the last 24 hours that might have caused this?).
- Verify power status, fiber connectivity, and hardware health.

Step 5: Apply Known Fix or Workaround
- Search the Known Error Database (KEDB) for matching issues.
- If a known fix exists, apply it and monitor for 15 minutes.
- If no known fix, proceed to Step 6.

Step 6: Engage Vendor Support (if needed)
- If the issue involves vendor equipment (Ericsson, Nokia, Cisco):
  - Open a vendor support case with severity matching our classification.
  - Provide logs, alarms, and topology diagrams.
  - Vendor SLA: Severity 1 response within 30 minutes.

6. COMMUNICATION PROTOCOL

Internal Communication:
- Severity 1: Status updates every 30 minutes on the bridge call.
- Severity 2: Status updates every 60 minutes via email to stakeholders.
- Severity 3-4: Updates in the ServiceNow ticket.

Customer Communication:
- Customer Care team is notified via the "Mass Outage" alert in CRM.
- Automated outage notification sent to affected customers via SMS/app push.
- Customer-facing outage page updated at status.telecomco.com.
- Social media team prepared with holding statements.

Executive Communication:
- Severity 1: VP of Network Operations notified within 30 minutes.
- CTO briefed within 1 hour for Severity 1 events.
- Board notification required for outages exceeding 8 hours or affecting >1M customers.

7. RESOLUTION AND RECOVERY

Step 7: Implement Fix
- Apply the resolution (configuration change, hardware replacement, reroute).
- Monitor key metrics for 30 minutes post-fix:
  - Packet loss < 0.1%
  - Latency within baseline ± 10%
  - All monitoring alarms cleared

Step 8: Service Verification
- Conduct end-to-end testing from affected area.
- Confirm with Customer Care that complaint volume has returned to baseline.
- Get NOC Manager sign-off before closing the bridge call.

8. POST-INCIDENT REVIEW

Step 9: Root Cause Analysis (RCA)
- RCA must be completed within 5 business days for Severity 1-2.
- RCA document must include:
  - Timeline of events
  - Root cause determination
  - Contributing factors
  - Corrective actions with owners and due dates
- RCA is reviewed in the weekly Change Advisory Board (CAB) meeting.

Step 10: Lessons Learned
- Update the KEDB with new resolution procedures.
- If a process gap is identified, update this SOP.
- Share findings in the monthly NOC All-Hands meeting.

9. TOOLS AND SYSTEMS
- Monitoring: SolarWinds NPM, IBM Netcool, Grafana dashboards
- Ticketing: ServiceNow (ITSM module)
- Communication: Microsoft Teams (NOC Bridge channel), PagerDuty
- Documentation: Confluence (Network Documentation space)
- Change Management: ServiceNow Change module

10. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
- NOC Engineer (Tier 1): Alert triage, initial assessment, known fixes
- NOC Engineer (Tier 2): Advanced troubleshooting, vendor coordination
- NOC Manager: Escalation management, executive communication
- Field Technician: On-site hardware assessment and replacement
- Customer Care: Customer communication and complaint management
